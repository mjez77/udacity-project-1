How Macram√© helped me to deal with the stress and pressure caused by pandemic.

"When we are mindful,
deeply in touch with the present moment,
our understanding of what is going on deepens,
and we begin to be filled with acceptance,
joy, peace and love."
Thich Nhat Hanh

Macrame has helped me to deal with the stress and pressure caused by pandemic.

I did lose my job last year and my self-esteem suffered. Many members of my family and friends got the virus and some even lost the fight with it. It has been a very difficult time for me on a personal level. I have not been directly affected by the virus, but it has had its influence on my mental health. My freedom was restricted. I couldn't go out when I wanted, and I couldn't go where I wanted. At times it was depression, other times it was anxiety as fear and uncertainty ruled over my mind. I know that without macrame things would have been worst.

Looking on the bright side of things, my macrame craft really thrived last year. I had more free time to learn and practice the craft.

I also noticed that, when I was knotting, I was being transported away from tensions and confusions of everyday life. There was just me and my work. Paying more attention to the present moment - does it sound familiar? I was practicing mindfulness.

Crafting requires full focus and concentration. Creating macrame pieces, did not only disconnect me from the world around, but helped me to observe my thoughts and feelings and better understand myself. In result it was calming me down and making me happy.

Completing entire macrame projects would give me a great sense of accomplishment and it would also be a form of expression of my inner feelings, the ones I was not comfortable to talk about.
